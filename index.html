Living with Congenital Heart Disease

By: Asmy Fayad

Nov. 24, 2022

What is Congenital Heart Disease?

Congenital heart disease (CHD) refers to a group of cardiac conditions that manifest at birth. Based on the appearance of the blood arteries and the heart, there are numerous forms of CHD with varying degrees of severity. The heart chambers could be missing or deformed, which could lead to problems with breathing and even thinking.

How many people are born with CHD in the United States?

Experts say:

Approximately every year 40,000 babies are born with CHD in the United States. They are born with different kinds of heart issues and the need of a heart surgery to have a better health condition. As The Children’s Heart Foundation stated, “Even with improved treatments, people with CHDs are not cured even if their heart defect has been repaired. People with a CHD can develop other health problems over time, depending on their specific heart defect, the number of heart defects they have, and the severity of their heart defect.”
